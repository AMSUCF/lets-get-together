# Let's Get Together

A small **Bitsy-inspired** browser game adapted from Isaac Asimov's 1957 short
story *"Let's Get Together"* (included here as `asimov.txt`; the text is in the
public domain — no evidence the U.S. copyright was renewed).

You play **Elias Lynn**, Chief of the Bureau of Robotics. Security tells you
that ten humanoid robots have slipped into the country, each carrying a fragment
of a Total Conversion bomb. Apart they are harmless. Should they ever *get
together*, a city dies. Walk the offices and labs, interrogate the people around
you, and learn — too late, perhaps — what the phrase really means.

## How to play

Open `index.html` in any modern browser. No build step, no dependencies, no
network — all art is drawn from code.

### Controls

| Action | Keys |
| --- | --- |
| Move | Arrow keys or `W A S D` |
| Talk | Walk into a person |
| Scan / Continue | `Z` or `Space` |
| Replay (on the end screen) | `R` |

## How it plays out

1. **Cheyenne — the office.** Breckenridge briefs you on the humanoids.
2. **The robotics lab.** Laszlo gives the key clue: a positronic copy of a mind
   is never exact, so a humanoid *can* be told from a human.
3. **Washington.** The officials sketch the countermeasure — an all-Science
   conference at Cheyenne — and a certain phrase gets planted.
4. **Back at Cheyenne.** The realization lands. You invoke martial law and the
   **scanner** comes online (press `Z` while facing someone).
5. **The conference hall.** Scan the arriving scientists. Find every humanoid
   before any two can get together — then go and confront the man who sent them.

The ending stays faithful to the story's twist: *not blood, but high-grade
machine oil.*
